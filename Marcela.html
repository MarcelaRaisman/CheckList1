Section A

Clarity

Question 1 2 3 4 Reality check
If someone asks what you do, can you explain it in one clear sentence, without rambling or changing the explanation depending on who you're talking to? If you say "it depends," clarity is probably weak.
Are you actively choosing your target client, or mostly taking whoever says yes? If your clients look completely different from each other, you are probably reacting, not choosing.
Is your offer consistent, or are you customizing every proposal from scratch? If every deal starts from a blank page, scaling will be harder.
In the last 6 months, have you stayed focused, or changed direction several times? Frequent shifts usually signal pressure, not strategy.
Section B

Clients & Revenue

Question 1 2 3 4 Reality check
If one major client leaves tomorrow, does the business remain stable? If one client can shake the business, there is too much dependency.
Do you know which clients are actually profitable after time, complexity, and effort are factored in? Revenue alone does not tell the real story.
Can you clearly explain where your next 5 clients are likely to come from? "Word of mouth" is helpful, but it is not a full growth plan.
Do you say yes to work you know is not ideal, simply to keep revenue flowing? That usually points to short-term pressure driving decisions.
Section C

Operations

Question 1 2 3 4 Reality check
If you step away for a week, does work continue smoothly without you? If not, you are still acting as the system.
Do you have clear, repeatable processes, or does work rely on “how we usually do it”? If it lives mostly in people’s heads, it is fragile.
When demand increases, can the business absorb it without stress and confusion? Growth should stretch the business, not throw it into chaos.
How often are you fixing mistakes, delays, or miscommunication? Frequent firefighting usually means the process is not under control.
Section D

Structure & Control

Question 1 2 3 4 Reality check
Do you know your margins by project, service, or client, not just top-line revenue? Revenue is visible. Margins reveal reality.
Can you quickly see what is working and what is not, without digging for hours? If visibility takes too long, decision-making gets weaker.
Are important decisions based on clear numbers and evidence, not only intuition and urgency? Instinct matters, but data gives discipline.
When problems emerge, do you see them early or only when they become urgent? Late visibility is one of the most expensive habits in business.
Section E

Leadership

Question 1 2 3 4 Reality check
Do you have regular time to think about the business, not only work inside it? If all your time goes to execution, direction suffers.
Are you involved in most decisions, including small ones? That often means you have become the bottleneck.
Do you feel in control of the business, or does the business feel like it controls you? This answer usually says more than any metric.
Do you have a clear picture of what the next stage should look like? Without that picture, growth becomes random rather than strategic.

How to Use This Questionnaire

Rate each statement based on what is true today, not on what you wish were true. Be honest, not optimistic.

  • 1 = Not true at all
  • 2 = Rarely true
  • 3 = Mostly true
  • 4 = Completely true
Scoring

Scoring and Interpretation

Total score: add all 20 answers together. Minimum score: 20. Maximum score: 80.

60-80

You’re ready to scale. The foundation is relatively solid. The risk is not growth itself, but scaling without a clear direction.

45-59

You’re growing, but at risk. The business can move forward, but pressure will start exposing weak points.

20-44

You’re not ready to scale. More growth at this stage will likely create more chaos, dependency, and stress.

Important: look at your lowest-scoring section as well. That is usually where the business will break first under pressure.

Next Step

What To Do Next

1

If you scored high: Scale deliberately. Don’t just grow faster, grow smarter.

2

If you scored mid: Before pushing growth, fix your weak points. That’s where your risk is.

3

If you scored low: Pause the push for growth. Build structure first, then scale.

Scaling does not fix weak foundations, it amplifies them.
